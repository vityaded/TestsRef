Category 1: Job Vocabulary

  • 100: This adjective describes a job that is difficult but in an interesting way.
    Answer: What is challenging?
  • 200: This term describes a job with a standard schedule, typically from Monday to Friday during the day.
    Answer: What is nine-to-five?
  • 300: This verb means to get money in exchange for work.
    Answer: What is “to earn”?
  • 400: This prefix is used to mean “wrongly” or “badly,” as in to misunderstand something.
    Answer: What is “mis-”?
  • 500: This is a general term for a person who creates things, such as buildings, software, or fashion.
    Answer: What is a designer or architect (or similar creative roles)?

Category 2: Future Tenses

  • 100: Which future form (“will” or “going to”) is typically used for spontaneous decisions made at the moment of speaking?
    Answer: What is “will”?
  • 200: If you see dark clouds in the sky, you would say, “It’s _______ rain.”
    Answer: What is “going to”?
  • 300: Complete the sentence for a plan you’ve already made: “I _______ visit my grandparents this weekend.”
    Answer: What is “am going to”?
  • 400: Which form is most common for making general predictions or promises about the future?
    Answer: What is “will”?
  • 500: Adding this adverb makes a future plan sound much more certain: “I will _______ apply for that job.”
    Answer: What is “definitely”?

Category 3: Cause & Effect

  • 100: In the sentence, “If it rains, we will stay inside,” which part is the cause (or condition)?
    Answer: What is “If it rains”?
  • 200: This type of sentence structure, often using “if,” is used to talk about the future result of a possible action.
    Answer: What is the first conditional?
  • 300: Combine these ideas into one sentence: 1. You don’t hurry. 2. You will be late.
    Answer: What is “If you don’t hurry, you will be late”?
  • 400: Does the tense in the “if” part of a first conditional sentence refer to the present or the future?
    Answer: What is the present tense?
  • 500: What word connects the two ideas in this sentence: “He’s a great employee, _______, he’s always on time.”
    Answer: What is “because” (or “as,” “since”)?

Category 4: Work Life

  • 100: This is an advantage of working on a team.
    Answer: What is collaboration or sharing ideas (or similar answers)?
  • 200: This is an adjective for a job that makes you feel tired and stressed.
    Answer: What is stressful or tiring?
  • 300: The desire to succeed and achieve great things is called this.
    Answer: What is ambition?
  • 400: This is a potential downside of a job where you work outdoors.
    Answer: What is bad weather?
  • 500: Going straight to work after school allows you to do this, which you can’t do if you go to university first.
    Answer: What is earn money immediately?

Category 5: Grammar Grab Bag

  • 100: This is the pronoun you would use to replace “the heavy bag.”
    Answer: What is “it”?
  • 200: What is the negative form of “I will go”?
    Answer: What is “I won’t go”?
  • 300: This punctuation mark is often needed when an “if” clause starts a sentence.
    Answer: What is a comma?
  • 400: This word is used to show a contrast, as in: “The job is hard; _______, it’s very rewarding.”
    Answer: What is “however” or “nevertheless”?
  • 500: The “past simple” is used to talk about actions that are in what state?
    Answer: What is finished or completed?
